Low-Vision Rehabilitation Training

Unity C# VBA
Website

Graduate research project at NJIT’s MIXR Lab that leveraged Virtual Reality for Low-Vision Rehabilitation Training, aiming to improve orientation and mobility skills in a safe, controlled environment.

Developed collaboratively in a team of three, including a Ph.D. candidate and a Master’s student.

Primary Contributions:

  • Programmed realistic NPC behavior to simulate real-world pedestrian environments
  • Designed and developed street-crossing maps aligned with key research objectives
  • Implemented data logging to capture user interaction patterns for graduate analysis
  • Collaborated with graduate researchers to refine simulation goals and test scenarios
